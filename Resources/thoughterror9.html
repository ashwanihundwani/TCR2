"Should" Statements


When you say “should” and “shouldn’t” to try to get yourself to do hard tasks. These statements tend to make you feel guilty and controlled by others. Also included are statements with the words “must” and “ought.” Sometimes, a good substitution is the word “choose.”


Example: “I should not eat this hamburger and have a salad instead.”


Tinnitus Example: “I should not have to deal with tinnitus during the best years of my life.”


Corrected Thought: “Tinnitus isn’t what I expected when I retired, however, I choose to live a valued life despite it.”
